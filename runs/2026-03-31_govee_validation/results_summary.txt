Govee vs Pi/Nano Environmental Validation Summary

Test window:
2026-03-31 14:02 to 2026-03-31 17:02

Data handling:
- Pi/Nano raw log was filtered to the same overlapping time window as the Govee export.
- Pi/Nano data were averaged to 1-minute intervals to match the Govee sampling period.
- Temperature and humidity were compared using overlay plots, scatter plots, MAE, RMSE, and bias.

Results:
- Rows merged: 181
- Temperature MAE: 1.822 °C
- Temperature RMSE: 1.885 °C
- Temperature Bias: +1.665 °C

- Humidity MAE: 3.647 %
- Humidity RMSE: 3.809 %
- Humidity Bias: -3.164 %

Interpretation:
- The Pi/Nano and Govee measurements showed broadly similar time-varying trends.
- However, a clear systematic offset was present.
- The Pi/Nano temperature readings were generally higher than the Govee readings.
- The Pi/Nano humidity readings were generally lower than the Govee readings.
- Agreement worsened during periods of rapid environmental change.
- This suggests the Pi/Nano sensing chain is suitable for trend monitoring and threshold-based alerts, but not yet calibrated for close agreement with the reference logger.
